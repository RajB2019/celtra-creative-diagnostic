# Celtra Creative Diagnostic Tool — Product Write-Up

---

## 1. Target User & Core Problem

**Who:** Performance marketers, creative strategists, and marketing executives at brands running multi-format paid campaigns across Meta, TikTok, and similar platforms. They work inside tools like Celtra Studio, Meta Ads Manager, and TikTok Business Center — but use none of them to answer the cross-cutting question: *why did some creatives perform and others didn't?*

**The decision they're trying to make:** What to produce next, what to scale, and what to cut — backed by evidence rather than instinct. Every sprint cycle, these teams need a defensible answer to "which creative choices are actually driving results?"

**The core problem:** Campaign dashboards tell you *what happened* — CTR was 2.3%, conversions were 410. They don't tell you *why* the 15-second video on Instagram outperformed the static on Facebook by 40%, or whether that difference is real or noise given the sample size. The result is creative decisions made by HiPPO (highest paid person's opinion), gut feel, or whoever argues loudest in the debrief.

---

## 2. Why Is This Problem Hard?

**Existing tools fall short in three specific ways:**

1. **Raw dashboards show metrics, not patterns.** Ads Manager gives you performance per creative. It doesn't tell you whether video outperforms static *in aggregate across your campaign*, how large that gap is, or whether you have enough data to act on it.

2. **Attribution tools answer media efficiency, not creative quality.** MMM and MTA models explain channel-level budget allocation. They don't decompose performance by creative attribute — format, copy length, visual style, CTA type.

3. **Creative reporting in production tools isn't diagnostic.** Celtra Studio and similar DCO platforms are production tools. Their analytics are designed to measure delivery and versioning, not to surface which creative *decisions* are driving or killing performance.

The gap is structural: the data exists in fragments across three or four platforms, and no one tool is designed to synthesize it into a brief-ready answer. Creative teams end up exporting CSVs, building one-off pivot tables, and doing the analysis across channels and campaigns.

---

## 3. Hypotheses Being Tested

This prototype is designed to validate several product hypotheses:

1. **Directional confidence tiers are more useful than significance tests at campaign scale.** Most creative sets have 30–80 records — too few for valid p-values. The hypothesis is that practitioners prefer honest directional signals (Strong / Moderate / Weak / Noise) over technically-framed statistics that would be misleading at this N.

2. **Persona-specific framing reduces time-to-action.** The same insight — "video outperforms static by 22% with strong confidence" — means different things to a budget owner vs. a brief writer. The hypothesis is that pre-framing by role reduces the interpretation step and shortens the path from data to decision.

3. **Connected system actions are the missing bridge.** Insights without next steps sit in decks. The hypothesis is that embedding deep-links into Celtra Studio, Ads Manager, and TikTok Business Center — contextually filtered per insight and persona — meaningfully reduces the friction between "I see the pattern" and "I did something about it."

4. **Lifecycle signals change the creative cut decision.** A creative with a declining trend and high spend is a different problem than a new creative with low impressions. The hypothesis is that surfacing lifecycle alongside performance score (rather than just ranking by CTR) changes which creatives teams decide to cut vs. give more runway.

5. **Noise findings are as valuable as strong ones.** If copy length shows no effect in this dataset, that's a real finding. The hypothesis is that practitioners will use "this attribute doesn't matter here" to focus effort, not just dismiss it as a missing result.

---

## 4. Key Assumptions & Limitations

**Assumptions:**

- Creative attribute metadata (format, copy length, visual style, CTA type) is available and accurately labeled in the source data. In practice, this data quality is highly variable.
- Performance is measured by a single normalized score (percentile within platform × funnel stage peers). This collapses a multi-dimensional performance picture into one number — a tradeoff that favors clarity over nuance.
- Peer group comparison is valid at the customer level. The tool compares creatives within one account's export, not against industry benchmarks. This is intentional but limits generalizability.

**Limitations:**

- **No real data ingestion.** The dataset is baked into the JS bundle — 40 real records plus ~40 synthetic records generated by a seeded LCG. There is no CSV upload, no API connection, no live campaign data.
- **Connected actions are simulated.** The deep-link buttons demonstrate the UX pattern but do not trigger live API calls into Celtra Studio.
- **Single campaign scope.** The diagnostic is designed for one creative set. Cross-account benchmarking is out of scope.
- **No statistical significance.** Confidence tiers are directional heuristics (N threshold + absolute delta), not inferential statistics. This is appropriate given sample sizes but must be communicated clearly to users.
- **Desktop only.** Designed for 1280px+ screens. Mobile is a non-goal for this prototype.
- **No persistence.** Filter state and persona selection are lost on page refresh.

## 5. MVP Scope & Non-Goals

**In scope:**
- Normalized performance scoring (percentile within platform × funnel stage peers)
- Nine attribute breakdown panels with confidence tiers
- Lifecycle classification (ramping / peak / evergreen / declining / stale) and trend signals
- Spend and CPM as selectable metrics alongside impression-based scoring
- 21 insight rules covering creative attributes, spend efficiency, and lifecycle patterns
- Persona-adaptive framing across three roles
- Connected system action mapping per insight type and persona
- Full 5-step diagnostic flow: Overview → Patterns → Reliability → Recommendations → Summary

**Intentionally not solved:**
- Live data ingestion (CSV upload, API integration)
- Authentication, user accounts, or data isolation
- Cross-campaign or industry benchmarking
- Export functionality (PDF, shareable links)
- Creative preview (thumbnail / video rendering)
- Mobile or tablet layout
- Live API calls to Celtra Studio
- Statistical significance testing

---

## 6. Workflow Fit

This tool is designed to live at the **post-flight / pre-brief boundary** of the creative production cycle — after a campaign has run long enough to generate signal, and before the team sits down to brief the next wave of creative.

In practice, that means it's used in two moments:

1. **The creative review.** The Performance Marketer pulls the campaign export, loads the diagnostic, and uses the Overview and Patterns tabs to identify what worked and what didn't. The tool replaces the ad-hoc pivot table built in a spreadsheet the day before by short circuiting the path from data analysis to insight while retaining basic data exploration capabilities.

2. **The brief kickoff.** The Creative Strategist uses the Patterns tab and Recommendations tab to translate performance data into creative hypotheses — which formats to double down on, which attributes to test in the next flight, which creatives to retire. The connected action buttons bridge directly into Celtra Studio to start the next production cycle.

The Executive Summary tab serves a third, lighter-touch moment: the weekly or monthly stakeholder read-out where a CMO or VP needs a one-screen answer to "is our creative working?"

## 7. Success Metrics

**Leading indicators (adoption and engagement):**

- Time from data load to first recommendation acted on (proxy for path-to-action friction)
- Percentage of connected action CTAs clicked per session (measures whether the insight-to-action bridge is used)

**Lagging indicators (business impact):**

- Change in creative iteration velocity: do teams brief faster after using the tool?
- Change in creative performance over subsequent flights: does acting on diagnostic recommendations improve CTR / CVR / CPA on the next wave?
- Reduction in time spent building ad-hoc creative performance reports (qualitative, measured via user interviews)

**Validation signals for the core hypotheses:**

- Do practitioners cite confidence tiers when justifying creative decisions? (Tests hypothesis 1)
- Do different personas take different actions from the same underlying data? (Tests hypothesis 2)
- Are noise findings referenced in debriefs, or ignored? (Tests hypothesis 5)

---

## 8. Technical Architecture (Summary)

100% static — React, Vite, Tailwind, no backend, no API calls. Hash-routed for three persona URLs that open correctly without a server. The engine layer is strictly pure functions: `metrics.js`, `insights.js`, `timeseries.js`, and `connectedSystems.js` have no React imports and no side effects. Insights flow one-way through the UI; persona affects framing and connected actions only, never the underlying scores or patterns.

The architecture is designed to be replaced at the data layer: swap the embedded dataset for an API call, wire the connected action buttons to live endpoints, and the rest of the system — scoring, patterns, confidence tiers, persona framing — carries forward unchanged.
